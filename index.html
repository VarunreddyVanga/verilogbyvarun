VERILOG

Verilog, standardized as IEEE 1364, is a hardware description language (HDL) used to model electronic systems. It is most commonly used in the design and verification of digital circuits at the register-transfer level of abstraction.

Hardware Descriptive Language:

  • Classical designs uses manual method to design the digital circuits.
  • By manual method million transistor design cannot be designed.
  • HDL describes the hardware of digital System in textual form.
  • Working circuit can be easily synthesized.
  • HDL acts as an platform for several tools.

  • Synthesis can be carried out using Field programmable Gate Array's (FPGA) or Application Specific Integrated Circuit (ASIC).
  • FPGA provides programmability to implement any possible design.
  • FPGA are relatively cost effective and design flow is simple, but FPGA's are not optimized.
  • Less energy efficient, but performance is lower than ASIC.
  • ASIC are designed for Specific Application.
  • ASIC Design flow is complex and higher initial production cost.
  • ASIC are highly optimized and energy efficient.
  • ASIC are more likely for mass production.

Logic Values:

  • 0: zero, logic low, false,etc.
  • 1: one, logic high, power.
  • x: unknown.
  • z: high impedance.

Number representation in Verilog:

Declaration:
<size> <radix> <value> =

<size> = No.of Bits

<radix> = Number Representation

<value> = Consecutive value - O to f, x, z

  • Binary -> b (or) B
  • Octal -> o (or) O
  • Decimal -> d (or) D
  • HexaDecimal -> h (or) H

Ex1: 4'b 0101

Ex2: 8'h 0101xxxx

Ex3: 12'o 3zx7 = 011 zzz xxx 111
